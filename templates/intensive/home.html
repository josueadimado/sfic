Training & Mission

About Didasko Africa 2026

Didasko comes from the Greek word meaning "to learn, to teach," reflecting Christ's command to teach and disciple. This trip combines deliverance ministry training, spiritual equipping, prayer, worship, fellowship, outreach, and mission exposure in West Africa.

  • Field training and cross-cultural mission experience
  • Deliverance ministry training and spiritual formation
  • Prayer, worship, fellowship, and ministry activities
  • Outreach and mission opportunities
  • Safari and sightseeing experience in Northern Ghana

Host Organizations

Didasko 2026 is hosted in partnership with these ministries and conferences across West Africa.

WHAT YOU WILL EXPERIENCE

Didasko 2026 is a ten-day mission journey that combines biblical training, cross-cultural ministry, and an unforgettable safari adventure. Participants will be equipped for service while experiencing the beauty of West Africa and God's creation.

DIDASCO TRAINING & DELIVERANCE

Five days of deliverance ministry training, spiritual equipping, prayer, worship, fellowship, and outreach opportunities in Lomé, Togo.

CROSS-CULTURAL MISSION

Engage in field training and ministry alongside the Eastern Sahel Union Mission and Togo Conference in a vibrant West African context.

SAFARI & SIGHTSEEING

Three days exploring Mole National Park in Northern Ghana — game drives, wildlife viewing, scenic landscapes, and cultural encounters with local communities.

TRANSFORM LIVES

Train. Equip. Serve. Experience. Return home with practical ministry tools, deeper faith, and lasting memories from this mission adventure.

Africa Safari Adventure

Explore. Discover. Experience.

The final three days of Didasko 2026 take you to Mole National Park in Northern Ghana, Ghana's largest wildlife sanctuary, to witness the beauty of God's creation up close.

Game Drives

Guided drives through Mole National Park with experienced local guides.

Wildlife Viewing

Elephants, antelope, buffalo, warthogs, baboons, and a rich variety of bird species.

Scenic Landscapes

Experience the sweeping savanna and natural beauty of West Africa's premier wildlife reserve.

Cultural Encounters

Visit local communities and experience Ghanaian culture and hospitality firsthand.

Important travel notice

September falls during the rainy season in Northern Ghana. Wildlife may be more dispersed and not congregate at traditional watering holes, which can affect viewing opportunities.

We encourage flexible expectations and trust in God's timing.
